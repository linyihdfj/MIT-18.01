We already know quotient rule:
$$
\left(\frac{u}{v}\right)' = \frac{u'v - u(v')}{v^2}
$$
Now, find the $\frac{d}{dx}(\tan x)$ using quotient rule and remember $\tan x = \frac{\sin x}{\cos x}$

$$
\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{\sin x}{\cos x}\right) = \frac{\cos x \cos x - \sin x (-\sin x)}{\cos^2x} = \frac{\cos^2x + \sin^2 x}{\cos^2x} = \frac{1}{\cos^2 x}
$$
We use a trig identity: $\sin^2  x+ \cos^2x = 1$
